# HR Analysis Dashboard – Summary Report

## Overview

The **HR Analysis Dashboard** provides a comprehensive view of workforce composition, compensation trends, and employee demographics. This dashboard is designed to help **HR leaders and management make data-driven decisions** related to salary planning, departmental performance, and employee distribution.

## Key Metrics Summary

* **Total Employees:** 1,470
* **Total Monthly Income:** 9,559,309
* **Total Monthly Rate:** 21,040,262
* **Total Percent Salary Hike:** 22,358

---

## Department-wise Insights

* **R&D** is the largest and most impactful department:

  * Highest employee count
  * Highest total working years
  * Maximum monthly income contribution
* **Sales** ranks second in income and salary hikes, indicating performance-linked compensation.
* **HR** has the smallest workforce and income contribution, reflecting its support-function role.

---

## Education Level Analysis

* Employees with **Bachelor’s and Master’s degrees** contribute the highest monthly income.
* **Doctoral degree holders** form a smaller group with comparatively lower total income due to limited headcount.
* **High School–level employees** show moderate income contribution but receive lower salary hikes.

---

## Salary Hike Trends

* **R&D employees** receive the highest percentage of salary hikes, aligning with innovation-driven and high-skill roles.
* **Sales** shows strong hike patterns, supporting incentive-based performance growth.
* **HR salary hikes** remain comparatively stable.

---

## Travel & Gender Distribution

* The majority of employees **travel rarely**, especially female employees.
* **Frequent travel roles** are dominated by male employees, indicating role-based travel requirements.
* These insights can support improvements in **work-life balance and flexible work policies**.

---

## Education Field Income Contribution

* **Life Sciences** and **Medical** fields generate the highest monthly income.
* **Marketing** and **Technical Degree** fields show steady income contributions.
* **Human Resources** and **Other** fields contribute smaller income shares.

---

## Business Insights & Recommendations

* Invest more in **R&D talent retention** due to high performance and income contribution.
* Align **Sales incentives** more closely with revenue and performance targets.
* Review **salary hike policies** for underrepresented education groups.
* Use **travel pattern insights** to design flexible or hybrid work strategies.

---

## Conclusion

This HR dashboard enables effective **workforce planning** by combining compensation, education, and departmental analytics. It supports strategic HR decisions such as **salary optimization, talent investment, and employee engagement improvement**, demonstrating the power of analytics in modern HR management.
